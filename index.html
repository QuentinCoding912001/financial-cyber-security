Financial Cybersecurity Tips

By Quentin Bombande

Use Strong Unique Passwords

  • Create passwords with at least 10–12 characters
  • Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Avoid names, birthdays, or common words
  • Never reuse the same password across accounts

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Add an extra layer of security using a verification code, fingerprint, or facial recognition in addition to your password.

Never Share Sensitive Information

  • Do not share passwords, PINs, OTPs, or account details via calls, SMS, or email
  • Banks will never request sensitive information unexpectedly

Be Careful With Links and Attachments

  • Avoid clicking suspicious links or pop-ups
  • Always access your bank directly through the official website or app

Watch Out for Phishing Scams

Scammers often impersonate banks or trusted organizations. Always verify unexpected requests before responding.

Keep Your Devices Updated

  • Regularly update your phone, computer, and applications
  • Enable automatic updates where possible

Use Trusted Security Software

  • Install reputable antivirus and anti-malware tools
  • Keep them updated at all times

Use Secure Websites

  • Ensure the website starts with https://
  • Look for a padlock icon in the browser

Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking

  • Do not access financial accounts on public Wi-Fi
  • Use mobile data or trusted private networks instead

Use Only Your Personal Devices

  • Avoid logging into banking accounts on shared computers
  • Always log out after each session
